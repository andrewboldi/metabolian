How confidence is drawn

Every reaction in the atlas carries an explicit confidence level — the schema will not validate one without it. The surface encodes that level before you read it: better-grounded claims sit proud of the field and are ruled solid, weaker ones lie flat and go dashed.

High
Textbook or curated-database consensus. Resolves to a primary registry identifier.
Medium
Well-supported primary literature. The chemistry is settled; the identifier may not be.
Low
A single source, or preliminary. Read it as a lead, not a fact.
Hypothesis
Model-suggested and unverified. Drawn flat and dashed. Do not cite it.

Source register

Nothing on this sheet is generated. Each reaction declares at least one source, and every accession it carries links back to the registry that asserts it.

  • Rhea

    Reaction equations, direction, and stoichiometry.

  • ChEBI

    Metabolite identity, formula, and structure.

  • MetaNetX

    Reconciliation of compound and reaction IDs across registries.

  • KEGG

    Reference reaction and pathway map identifiers.

  • Reactome

    Curated human pathway structure and ordering.

  • UniProt

    Enzyme identity, gene symbol, and EC assignment.

  • PubMed

    Primary literature for tissue-specific and newly characterized steps.

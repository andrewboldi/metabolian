About

A living successor to the Biochemical Pathways chart

In the 1960s Gerhard Michal drew metabolism by hand for Boehringer/Roche. Metabolian carries that work forward as an open, interactive graph you can query, audit, and extend.

How the atlas is built

Metabolian is a graph. Each pathway module is a structured file describing its metabolites, enzymes, reactions, and — crucially — the typed relationships between them. A build step validates every module against a strict schema, checks that every reference resolves, and merges the modules into one master graph, deduplicating shared molecules across pathways by their database identifiers.

Grounded, not guessed. Metabolism is too consequential to hallucinate. Every reaction and regulatory link carries provenance — the database (KEGG, Reactome, Rhea, UniProt, ChEBI) or paper it came from — and an explicit confidence level: high medium low hypothesis. You can see and check the source of anything on the chart.

The schema & the grammar of arrows

What makes a pathway chart legible is that its arrows mean something. Metabolian defines a distinct, color-coded arrow for every relationship type — substrate and product, catalysis, cofactor use, activation and the many flavors of inhibition, allostery, feedback, covalent modification, transport, transcriptional control, and cross-pathway talk. The full taxonomy is the grammar of metabolism, and the same registry drives the legend, the interactive graph, and this documentation.

Reactions record substrates and products with stoichiometry and compartment, catalyzing enzymes with EC numbers, cofactors, reversibility, thermodynamics and kinetics where known, and links to KEGG/Rhea/Reactome. Enzymes carry gene symbols, UniProt accessions, and structure links so any enzyme opens directly in the 3D viewer via AlphaFold and the PDB.

Data sources

  • KEGG — reaction and pathway reference identifiers.
  • Reactome & MetaCyc — curated human pathway structure.
  • Rhea & ChEBI — reaction and chemical ontology.
  • UniProt — enzyme identity, gene, and cross-references.
  • AlphaFold DB & RCSB PDB — 3D protein structures.
  • Primary literature — for newly characterized and tissue-specific reactions.

How it's engineered

A TypeScript multi-page app built with Vite; the interactive chart renders with a WebGL-accelerated graph engine and loads heavy modules only where needed. Structures render with 3Dmol.js; the home page's ambient graph is Three.js with reduced-motion respected everywhere. Data is validated in CI on every change, and the whole site deploys to GitHub Pages.

Not medical advice. Metabolian is an educational and research aggregation. It is not a clinical reference and must not be used to make medical, dietary, or treatment decisions.

Contribute

Authoring a pathway means writing one module file against the schema, with citations and honest confidence. Issues and pull requests are welcome on GitHub.

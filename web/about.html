How the atlas is built, and what it does not yet know

Metabolian is an open successor to Gerhard Michal's Biochemical Pathways chart — 2,496 pathway modules merged into one queryable graph. Every reaction carries the source it was drawn from and an explicit confidence. This page states the method, the exact sources, and the gaps.

01How the atlas is built

Metabolian is a graph assembled from files. Each pathway module is one JSON document describing its own metabolites, enzymes, reactions, and — the part that makes a chart legible — the typed relationships between them. One pathway per file, so two authors are never editing the same document.

A build step validates every module against the schema, checks that every internal reference resolves, then merges all 2,496 modules into a single master graph, deduplicating shared molecules across pathways by their database identifiers. Nothing reaches the site that has not passed validation in CI.

Pipeline

Grounded, not guessed. Metabolism is too consequential to hallucinate. Every reaction and regulatory link carries provenance — the database or paper it came from — and an explicit confidence level. All 26,771 reaction and regulation records in the current build carry at least one source; there are no exceptions, because the validator rejects them.

02Where the data comes from

The honest headline is that this atlas has two tiers, and they are not the same thing. Twenty-seven modules are hand-curated: they declare organisms, tissues, and compartments, they carry regulation, cross-pathway relations, and literature references. The remaining 2,469 are machine-reconciled reaction sets — balanced chemistry with resolved identifiers, but no regulation, no compartments, and no tissue context.

A cross-reference is not a citation

Metabolian keeps the two apart, and so should you when you read an entry. A cross-reference tells you what a thing is — ChEBI names the chemical, the EC list names the catalytic activity. A citation tells you why we believe a reaction happens. ChEBI appears everywhere on this site and is never used as evidence.

03What confidence means

Every reaction and regulation carries one of four levels. They describe how well established a claim is, not how important it is.

The atlas is overwhelmingly high-confidence, and that is a statement about its provenance, not its completeness. It is assembled from reconciled reaction databases where the chemistry has already been balanced and cross-checked. The lower tiers exist for the hand-curated biology being added now: 54 of the 224 regulatory links are already medium, because a regulatory claim from one paper is not the same kind of fact as a balanced reaction from Rhea.

04The schema and the grammar of arrows

What makes a pathway chart legible is that its arrows mean something. Metabolian defines 30 relationship types across 7 categories in a single registry — mass flow, catalysis, regulation, covalent modification, transport, genetic control, and organizational links — each with one authoritative line style, arrowhead, and colour.

That registry is the law: it drives the legend, the chart renderer, and this documentation from the same file, so a colour can never mean two things. The full taxonomy is the grammar of metabolism, and Learn shows the regulatory subset drawn at actual size.

Reactions record substrates and products with stoichiometry and compartment, catalysing enzymes with EC numbers, cofactors, reversibility, and thermodynamics or kinetics where known. Enzymes carry EC numbers almost universally; the small subset that also carries a UniProt accession opens directly in the 3D structure viewer via AlphaFold and the PDB.

05Known limits

A reference work should state its own coverage. These are measured, not estimated, and they are the gaps we would most like closed.

  • Regulation is thin. The arrow grammar for regulation is complete; the data is not. There are 224 regulatory links against 26,510 reactions, and all of them live in the 27 curated modules.
  • Structures are sparse. 383 enzyme records carry a UniProt accession, so the 3D viewer opens for a small subset of enzymes rather than all of them.
  • Sourcing is concentrated. 96% of citations are Rhea or MetaNetX. That is good evidence of balanced chemistry and weak evidence of physiological context.
  • Context is missing outside the curated tier. Organism, tissue, and compartment annotations exist in 27 of 2,496 modules. Treat an uncurated reaction as chemistry that occurs somewhere, not as chemistry that occurs in your cell type.

Not medical advice. Metabolian is an educational and research aggregation. It is not a clinical reference and must not be used to make medical, dietary, or treatment decisions.

06How it is engineered

  • A Vite multi-page app in vanilla TypeScript. No framework. Each page ships only its own JavaScript, and heavy libraries are dynamically imported so they never land in a bundle that does not use them.
  • The pathway sheet is drawn as SVG, not a canvas — real text, real DOM, so browser find-in-page, text selection, and screen readers all work on the chart. An anti-collision placer positions labels against measured glyph extents.
  • Explore is semantic DOM rather than a node-link canvas, for the same reason: a compound's neighbourhood is a ledger, and DOM gives it real focus order and keyboard navigation for free.
  • Protein structures render with 3Dmol.js, loaded only on the structure page.
  • Type is self-hosted and subset. The site makes no external network request — no CDN, no font service, no analytics.
  • Motion is opt-in. Nothing on this site animates for a reader whose system asks for reduced motion.
  • Data is validated in CI on every change, and the whole site deploys as static files to GitHub Pages.

07Contribute

Authoring a pathway means writing one module file against the schema, with citations and an honest confidence on every claim. Because it is one file per pathway, your work never collides with anyone else's. Validate a single module with node tools/validate.mjs data/pathways/<id>.json before opening a pull request.

The most valuable contributions right now are the ones that close the gaps above: regulation, tissue context, and UniProt accessions for enzymes that have none. Issues and pull requests are welcome on GitHub.
